# User Personas — SpartaFlow Hub

Five primary personas drive the design of SpartaFlow Hub. Each persona represents a real role within SpartaFlow with distinct goals, frustrations, and success criteria.

---

## 1. Adrian — The Owner

- **Age:** 38
- **Role:** Founder & CEO
- **Location:** Remote, travels frequently
- **Tools today:** ClickUp, Slack, Google Workspace, spreadsheets

**Goals**
- Understand the operational health of the entire company in one glance.
- Spot underperforming or overloaded departments early.
- Make informed decisions on hiring, restructuring, and project bets.
- Trust that the company runs without his daily intervention.

**Frustrations**
- Has to ping managers to understand status.
- Gets surprised by missed deadlines that "everyone knew about."
- Operational data lives in too many places.

**What he needs from SpartaFlow Hub**
- A company health dashboard (attendance, blockers, dependencies, engagement).
- Department-level performance comparisons over time.
- A single weekly digest summarizing the operational state.

**Success looks like**
> "I open SpartaFlow Hub once a day for 60 seconds and I know exactly where the company stands."

---

## 2. Lina — HR Lead

- **Age:** 32
- **Role:** Head of People
- **Location:** Remote

**Goals**
- Keep attendance and leave data accurate and auditable.
- Maintain a live employee directory across 12 departments.
- Communicate company-wide announcements with confidence they were read.
- Onboard and offboard employees smoothly.

**Frustrations**
- Chasing attendance and leave information manually.
- Announcements get lost in chat channels.
- No reliable view of who is actually working today.

**What she needs from SpartaFlow Hub**
- HR dashboard with attendance, leaves, and late arrivals.
- Announcement tool with audience targeting and read tracking.
- Employee directory with filters.
- Exportable reports for management and compliance.

**Success looks like**
> "I never have to ask a manager 'who was late this week?' again."

---

## 3. Marco — Project Manager

- **Age:** 35
- **Role:** Project Manager, multiple cross-functional projects
- **Location:** Remote

**Goals**
- Know in real time which team members are working, blocked, or off.
- Resolve cross-department dependencies before they impact deadlines.
- Aggregate daily reports into project-level signals.
- Spend less time chasing status and more time unblocking people.

**Frustrations**
- Has to read 12 different chat channels to assemble a status update.
- Dependencies between Backend, Flutter, and AI are tracked verbally.
- Risks surface in retros, not in real time.

**What he needs from SpartaFlow Hub**
- Live team status board.
- Dependency board sorted by age and priority.
- Daily report rollups per project and per team.
- Escalation alerts when blockers age past a threshold.

**Success looks like**
> "Every morning I see exactly what's at risk and what I need to unblock — before standup."

---

## 4. Sara — Team Lead (Backend)

- **Age:** 30
- **Role:** Backend Team Lead, also writes code daily
- **Location:** Remote

**Goals**
- Run a healthy team without becoming a full-time status reporter.
- Spot when a team member is overloaded, blocked, or struggling.
- Coordinate dependencies with Flutter, Web, AI, and DevOps.
- Give honest performance feedback grounded in data.

**Frustrations**
- Context-switches constantly between coding and chasing updates.
- Sometimes learns about a blocker days after it started.
- Annual performance reviews rely on memory and gut feel.

**What she needs from SpartaFlow Hub**
- A team dashboard she can scan in 30 seconds.
- Visibility into who finished their report and who didn't.
- Per-person performance trends to support fair feedback.
- Easy way to log and resolve cross-team dependencies.

**Success looks like**
> "I trust my team's status without having to interrupt anyone."

---

## 5. Karim — Employee (Flutter Developer)

- **Age:** 26
- **Role:** Mid-level Flutter Developer
- **Location:** Remote, flexible hours

**Goals**
- Start the day with a clear plan.
- Know what's expected of him without being micromanaged.
- Communicate blockers and dependencies easily.
- Feel his work is visible and valued.

**Frustrations**
- Forgets to send daily reports when busy.
- Doesn't always know who to ask when blocked.
- Worries that quiet days look like lazy days.

**What he needs from SpartaFlow Hub**
- A simple guided daily flow that takes under 2 minutes.
- Easy way to raise a dependency on another team.
- Personal dashboard showing his streak, hours, and notifications.
- Confidence that submitting a report is enough — no one will chase him.

**Success looks like**
> "I follow the daily flow and the company already knows what I did. I focus on building."

---

## Secondary Persona — Viewer

A read-only stakeholder (e.g. external investor, advisor, or auditor) who needs limited visibility into specific dashboards without seeing personal data. Designed for tightly scoped, configurable access.
